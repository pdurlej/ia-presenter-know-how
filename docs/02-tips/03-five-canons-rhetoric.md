# The Five Canons of Rhetoric

> Reading time: 12 minutes

---

Great presentations aren't about polished formatting—it's about how to get your message across.

Presenting is an ancient art, studied and refined over centuries, and captured in the Five Canons of Rhetoric. These principles offer simple guidelines for the art of clear communication.

---

## 1. Idea

### Focus on Content, Not Appearance

The heart of a great presentation lies in the message itself, not in how it looks. Your slides are just visual aids to your storytelling.

> They may forget what you said, but they will never forget how you made them feel. ―Carl W. Buechner

**Storytelling is key.** Stories create connections. A connection isn't achieved through stock images, graphs, or bullet points, but through the power of your voice.

---

## 2. Order

### Structure Your Presentation

A clear flow keeps your audience engaged. Your structure is the framework. It gives your message order and makes it easy to follow.

### Simple Three-Part Structure

1. **Introduction (Set the Stage)**
   - Introduce your key message
   - Explain the problem or challenge
   - Show why the audience should care

2. **Development (Build the Case)**
   - Present your argument or story
   - Each point should connect to the next
   - Make sure your ideas flow logically

3. **Conclusion (Wrap Up)**
   - Summarize your key points
   - Reinforce the core message
   - Leave the audience with something to remember

### One Idea Per Slide

Stick to one idea per slide. Too many ideas on one slide can confuse the audience. Simple slides make your message clearer and easier to remember.

---

## 3. Style

### Find the Right Words

Simplify your sentences and find the right connecting words. Think about your audience. Speak their language.

### Design with Markdown

In slide presentations, both words and visuals need a good style. Many slide design mistakes come from too many options in programs like PowerPoint.

**Less is more.** Avoid walls of text and excessive bullet points. The audience is too busy digesting the information on the slide to listen to you.

### Choose Images Carefully

Images can be technically splendid, with great colors, lighting, and a wonderful landscape. But if they don't tell a good story they make a lot of noise without adding meaning.

---

## 4. Rehearse and Memorize

### Practice Makes Perfect

Ideally, you'll rehearse an important presentation three times from beginning to end. If it still sucks the third time, you're prepared.

Never be scared to talk to an audience that is willing to listen to you. Enjoy the privilege of the time they are willing to give you.

---

## 5. And... Action

### Finish on Time

Being afraid to speak in front of a crowd is normal. Everyone, even the most experienced speaker knows that fear in one or the other form.

The way to deal with this fear is to be prepared enough until a part of you is looking forward to present. Be so prepared that you are the person who is most informed on that specific thing you are going to talk about.
