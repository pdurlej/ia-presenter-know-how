# Behind the Gesture

> Reading time: 12 minutes

---

Should you use gestures while you're presenting? What about facial expressions? Find out in this vivid and still-relevant chapter.

Public speaking advice drowns us in rules about where to place your hands, how to move your feet, or when to raise your eyebrows. But authentic expression doesn't begin with the body. It begins with conviction. Gestures that matter aren't calculated, but felt.

---

## The Core Principle

Gesture is really a simple matter that requires observation and common sense rather than a book of rules. **Gesture is an outward expression of an inward condition.** It is merely an effect—the effect of a mental or an emotional impulse struggling for expression through physical avenues.

> "You must not, however, begin at the wrong end: if you are troubled by your gestures, or a lack of gestures, attend to the cause, not the effect."

If your thoughts and emotions are welling within you like a mountain spring, you will not have much trouble to make gestures. Gestures must be born, not built.

---

## What Makes a Good Gesture

### Gestures Should Born of the Moment

The best actors and public speakers rarely know in advance what gestures they are going to make. They make one gesture on certain words tonight, and none at all tomorrow night at the same point—their various moods and interpretations govern their gestures.

### Avoid Monotony in Gesture

Roast beef is an excellent dish, but it would be terrible as an exclusive diet. No matter how effective one gesture is, do not overwork it. Put variety in your actions. Monotony will destroy all beauty and power.

### Any Movement Not Significant Weakens

A great many useless movements will only take the attention of the audience from what you are saying. We naturally watch moving objects. A janitor putting down a window can take the attention of the hearers from Mr. Roosevelt.

---

## Facial Expression

Facial expression is important. The movements of the facial muscles may mean a great deal more than the movements of the hand. The eye has been called the window of the soul. Through it shines the light of our thoughts and feelings.

---

## Do Not Use Too Much Gesture

In fact, in the big crises of life we do not go through many actions. Fuss is not force. The barking dog never tears your trousers—at least they say he doesn't. Do not fear the man who waves his arms and shouts his anger, but the man who comes up quietly with eyes flaming and face burning may knock you down.

---

## Posture

The physical attitude to be taken before the audience really is included in gesture. Stand on the platform in the same easy manner that you would use before guests in a drawing-room. Do not stand with your heels close together, like a soldier or a butler. No more should you stand with them wide apart like a traffic policeman.

---

## Conclusion

So reiterate, do not despise practice. Your gestures and movements may be spontaneous and still be wrong. No matter how natural they are, it is possible to improve them.
