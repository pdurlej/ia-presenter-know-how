# How Can We Make Presentations Better?

> Reading time: 10 minutes

---

Great presentations are really great stories. It's great stories that move people, not stock images, bullet points, or diagrams. To achieve that we needed to create a new category of presentation apps.

---

## Yesterday: Look at the Past to Understand the Future

There's a simple, efficient, and fun way to prepare and tell a convincing story. It's called the Rhetoric Canon and it's the standard way to prepare a speech since ancient times:

| Step | Function |
|------|----------|
| 1. Idea | What do I say? |
| 2. Structure | Order and Orientation |
| 3. Style | Make yourself heard |
| 4. Practice | Rehearse and remember! |
| 5. Speech | Tell your story! |

---

## Harder, Better, Faster, Stronger: What We're Aiming At

We asked you what you've struggled with using PowerPoint. Your main concerns were that in traditional presentation apps you:

1. **Time:** Spend a lot of time without noticing and getting under time pressure
2. **Focus:** Struggle with design questions neglecting the content
3. **Confidence:** Fear of boring others or getting a blackout

---

## 1. Think: Idea

### Lose Yourself: Audience and Purpose

A presentation is not about us, our nerves, our insecurities, or the medium we happen to be using, it's not even about what we think. A presentation is about our audience.

### Great Presentations Are Great Stories

We think that great presentations are really great stories. And that it's great stories that move people, not stock images, bullet points, or diagrams.

### Fast Love: Work with Existing Text

The best way to start making a presentation is not with a template or an empty slide, but with a blank writing screen.

A blank writing screen removes the temptation to play with shapes and colors. It sets the tone for the task. Calm and clear.

---

## 2. Keep'Em Separated: Structure

We develop our narrative by writing and organizing our script. A script focused on what we want to say. A script that keeps our audience attentive, interested, and engaged because it's about them and the challenges they're facing.

---

## 3. Black or White: Visuals

Only when we've worked out exactly what we're going to say, do we think about what our audience is going to see while we're speaking to them.

Simple headlines and great illustrations reinforce what we're saying. They give the audience a visual hook to hang an idea on and keep them focused on the voice of the person presenting.

---

## 4. I Will Survive: Rehearsal

Let's think about the topic of practice for a second. When we practice our story properly two things are happening:

**Make it Your Own** - When we practice we're exposed to our own argument. Verify it. We'll hear our own voice. Listen to it.

**Ease Anxiety** - The more we edit and practice telling our story, the more confident we'll feel.

---

## 5. The Great Pretender: Delivery

While we're all-in on the benefits of proper practice, we didn't want to rely on it as a solution. We wanted a safety net, for when we're in the hot seat and all eyes are on us.

---

## What About Design?

### Responsive Design!

In times of responsive web design and mobile-first approaches, PowerPoint still forces us to obey strict resolutions and aspect ratios.

If you get a presentation on your phone, you should be able to read it like it's an email. No zooming, no pinching in and out.

### Let It Go: Automatic Layout!

Imagine not having to deal with layout at all. All you do is writing a text, editing on headlines, illustrating your story where it makes sense. The layout gets picked automatically.

---

## Easy Like Sunday Morning: Readable Text Documents as Handouts!

The story is the backbone of a good presentation. A story-based presentation app allows you to:

1. Import existing texts
2. To practice and optimize your pitch
3. To calm you down knowing that you have a safety net in case you get lost

Once the story works, you know it by heart and you almost don't need it anymore during the presentation.
