# Golden Deck Program

> How candidate decks in this repository should evolve into reusable Golden Deck patterns.

---

## What A Candidate Deck Is

A candidate deck is a working presentation draft used to test:
- narrative spine
- slide rhythm
- visible surface choices
- speaker-note density
- closing quality

Candidate decks are not treated as final examples just because they open correctly.

---

## Current Candidate Set

The repository currently includes candidate decks for:
- pitch
- board update
- workshop
- case study
- small smoke-test validation

These decks exist to expose weaknesses early, not to prove that the style is already finished.

---

## Review Loop

### Step 1: Open in iA Presenter

Do not review only in Markdown.

Check:
- slide thumbnails
- density
- layout rhythm
- visual repetition

### Step 2: Review visible surfaces

Ask:
- does each slide surface deserve to be shown?
- is the deck relying on one pattern too often?
- are the key lines visible enough?

### Step 3: Review notes

Ask:
- do notes sound sayable?
- do they add movement and nuance?
- are they deepening the slide instead of replacing it?

### Step 4: Review the close

Ask:
- does the deck end with a real action, decision, or line worth remembering?

### Step 5: Feed the learning back into the skill

If a pattern keeps failing, update:
- `skills/ia-presenter-deck/SKILL.md`
- the rubric
- the layout heuristics
- the slide surface patterns

The project should get better by updating the skill, not only by rewriting one deck at a time.

---

## Promotion Criteria

A candidate deck is ready to become a Golden Deck reference when:
- it survives multiple visual reviews in iA Presenter
- its rhythm no longer feels generic or repetitive
- the close works reliably
- its patterns are reusable beyond the original topic
- the skill can generate something similar on purpose

---

## Suggested Feedback Format

When reviewing a candidate, answer:
- strongest slide
- weakest slide
- where the flow drags
- where the visible slide text is too thin or too heavy
- where the notes are doing too much work
- which pattern should be reused
- whether the ending lands

---

## Project-Level Goal

The goal is not to preserve every candidate deck.

The goal is to learn which narrative moves and visible slide patterns are strong enough to encode into the canonical skill and eventually treat as Golden Deck references.
