# iA Presenter Deck Rubric

Use this rubric before delivery. The goal is to catch weak patterns early, not to maximize formality.

Score each item 0-10.

## 1. Goal Clarity

- Is the audience clear?
- Is the objective clear?
- Is the closing action or decision clear?

## 2. Story Spine

- Does the deck move with intention?
- Can you describe the narrative in one sentence?
- Do slides build on each other instead of feeling isolated?

## 3. Headline Strength

- Are titles specific and memorable?
- Do they avoid generic business labels?
- Are full assertions used only when they improve precision?

Penalize:
- Background
- Overview
- Key metrics
- Solution overview

## 4. Visible Slide Surface

- Does the audience-visible layer carry real meaning?
- Does the deck use more than one visible surface type when useful?
- Are important ideas shown, not only hidden in notes?

Penalize:
- slides that are effectively invisible apart from the title
- title-plus-tiny-bullets repeated over and over
- decks where all key substance lives in notes

## 5. Slide Density

- Is there enough information to justify the slide?
- Is there too much text for the slide to breathe?
- Would any weak split slides be stronger merged or reframed?

Penalize:
- empty two-column slides
- one short phrase stranded on half a slide
- memo-like bullet stacks

## 6. Layout Intent

- Does each slide clearly want to be stacked, compared, quoted, or image-led?
- Are side-by-side layouts used only when comparison adds meaning?
- Are tables used sparingly and only when the audience truly needs the grid?

## 7. Note-to-Slide Ratio

- Is the slide lean enough?
- Do the notes carry the real argument?
- Could the presenter actually deliver the slide naturally?

Penalize:
- notes that are too thin to present from
- slides that contain the whole argument instead of supporting it
- notes that carry all substance because the visible slide is underdesigned

## 8. Speakability

- Do notes sound human and spoken?
- Are transitions natural?
- Does the deck have pacing, not just information?

## 9. Closing Quality

- Does the deck end with a real line, decision, or action?
- Does the ending feel earned?

Strong endings:
- one concrete next step
- one memorable line that compresses the deck
- one explicit decision request

Weak endings:
- Thank you
- Questions
- vague summary bullets

## 10. iA Presenter Fit

- Does the deck actually use iA Presenter conventions well?
- Are TAB-based slide lines used intentionally?
- Does the deck use more of iA Presenter than one repeated pattern?
- Would the deck likely render cleanly instead of feeling like a pasted PowerPoint script?

## 11. Candidate Distinction

Only score this when candidate mode is used.

- Are the directions genuinely different?
- Does each candidate express a different rhythm or rhetorical strategy?
- Is one direction obviously closest to Golden Deck quality?

## Delivery Threshold

- 8-10 on most items: ready
- repeated 5-6 scores: revise
- any 0-4 on headline strength, visible slide surface, closing quality, or iA Presenter fit: do not deliver as final
