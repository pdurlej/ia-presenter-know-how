# iA Presenter Templates

These are rhetorical templates, not rigid slide counts.
Pick one on purpose instead of drifting into the same deck shape every time.

## 1. Reframe-Led Pitch

1. cover
2. reframe
3. customer voice
4. tension or contrast
5. chapter-card reset
6. what changes
7. proof or pilot
8. decision / next step

Good surface mix:
- sharp cover
- one chapter card
- quote or customer-voice slide
- one crisp comparison
- one tiny proof table
- one closing landing line

## 2. Board / Executive Update

1. short version up front
2. situation / complication
3. our answer
4. what changed in the business
5. what we stopped doing
6. where we were wrong or what still worries us
7. proof of movement
8. risks and open questions
9. what we need
10. what success looks like next

Good surface mix:
- short-version slide
- one `SCQA` or answer-first opening move
- one short chapter-card or direct confession slide
- one before / after or stop / start contrast
- one exact full-width proof table
- one explicit ask slide

Rules:
- answer early
- build the body as a question chain
- use governing-thought headlines, not topic labels
- do not park the conclusion behind five context slides

## 3. Workshop / Teaching Deck

1. what this session gives you
2. the first mistake
3. the model or ladder
4. weak example / strong example
5. how to apply it
6. common traps
7. practice / action

Good surface mix:
- agenda
- one manifesto-style section reset
- weak / strong contrast
- ladder or sequence slide
- practice or checklist slide

## 4. Case Study / Story Deck

1. scene
2. real problem
3. before / after
4. what changed first
5. what we refused to do
6. result
7. what surprised us
8. what you can copy
9. closing line

Good surface mix:
- scene-setting cover or image
- before / after contrast
- one refusal or principle slide
- one visible takeaway line

## Reusable Slide Patterns

### Reframe

Use when the audience thinks the problem is one thing, but you need to shift the lens.

### Symmetry

Use one line on each side only when the contrast is genuinely crisp.

### One-Line Landing

Use for a closing or turning-point slide. One short line on slide. The argument lives in notes.

### Proof Table

Use only when a table is the fastest way to show movement. Keep it tiny.

### Sequence

Use when order matters. Prefer 3-4 steps, not 8.
