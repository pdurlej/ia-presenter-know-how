# iA Presenter Rhythm Maps

Use these when candidate mode starts producing cosmetic rewrites instead of genuinely different decks.

The point is not only to vary content. The point is to vary pacing, surface mix, and where the deck turns.

## 1. Reframe-Led Pitch

Best for:
- product pitches
- internal buy-in decks
- sales situations where the problem is misunderstood

Typical rhythm:
1. cover
2. reframe
3. customer voice
4. tension or contrast
5. chapter card
6. what changes
7. proof
8. decision
9. landing line

Visual mix:
- heading stack
- quote
- contrast
- tiny table
- strong close

## 2. Executive Update

Best for:
- board updates
- leadership reviews
- operational check-ins

Typical rhythm:
1. cover
2. short version
3. what changed
4. what stopped
5. where we were wrong
6. proof
7. risks
8. ask
9. close

Visual mix:
- compressed summary
- direct confession slide
- proof table
- ask slide

## 3. Workshop / Teaching

Best for:
- enablement
- training
- internal workshops

Typical rhythm:
1. cover
2. promise / agenda
3. first mistake
4. weak / strong contrast
5. model or ladder
6. checklist
7. practice
8. close

Visual mix:
- agenda
- reset slide
- contrast slide
- ordered ladder
- action checklist

## 4. Story / Case Study

Best for:
- case studies
- keynote segments
- internal transformation stories

Typical rhythm:
1. cover or scene
2. what made the old way expensive
3. before / after
4. first intervention
5. what we refused to do
6. result
7. what surprised us
8. takeaway
9. landing line

Visual mix:
- scene-setting line
- contrast
- proof
- refusal / principle slide
- takeaway close

## Selection Rule

If two candidates share:
- the same opening move
- the same slide silhouettes
- the same proof placement
- and the same kind of close

they are not truly different candidates.
